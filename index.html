Step by Step

Indian Election Process

campaign

Step 1 · ECI announces schedule

Election Announcement & Model Code of Conduct

The Election Commission of India (ECI) announces the election schedule. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) immediately comes into force, restricting ruling parties from using state resources for campaigning.

  • ECI sets phase-wise voting dates across constituencies
  • MCC prohibits new policy announcements & inaugurations
  • Notification published in the Official Gazette
how_to_reg

Step 2 · Voter rolls updated

Voter Registration & Electoral Roll

Citizens register on voter.eci.gov.in using Form 6. Electoral Rolls are maintained by BLOs (Booth Level Officers). The EPIC (Voter ID Card) is the primary identity document.

  • Age 18+ Indian citizens eligible to register
  • EPIC issued by ECI — 12 alternative documents accepted
  • Form 6 for new registration, Form 8 for corrections
assignment_ind

Step 3 · Candidate filing

Nomination, Scrutiny & Withdrawal

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. A ₹25,000 security deposit is required (₹12,500 for SC/ST). Nominations are scrutinized and candidates may withdraw by the withdrawal deadline.

  • Must be Indian citizen, age 25+ for Lok Sabha
  • Affidavit declaring criminal & financial records mandatory
  • Party symbols allotted to recognized parties
how_to_vote

Step 4 · Election Day

Voting via EVM & VVPAT

Voters cast ballots using Electronic Voting Machines (EVMs). Each EVM is connected to a VVPAT which prints a paper slip confirming the vote for 7 seconds. NOTA option is available for all voters.

  • Polling hours typically 7AM to 6PM
  • NOTA — None of the Above option available since 2013
  • Central paramilitary forces deployed for security
verified

Step 5 · Counting Day

Vote Counting & Result Certification

Counting begins at 8AM at counting centers under ECI observation. Postal ballots are counted first, then EVM votes. India uses First Past The Post (FPTP) — highest votes wins.

  • Postal ballots counted before EVM results
  • Security deposit forfeited below 1/6th of valid votes
  • ECI issues election certificate to the winner
