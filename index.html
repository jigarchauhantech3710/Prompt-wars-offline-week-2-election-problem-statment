Step by Step

Indian Election Process

campaign

Step 1 · ECI announces schedule

Election Announcement & Model Code of Conduct

The Election Commission of India announces the election schedule. The Model Code of Conduct (MCC) immediately comes into force, restricting parties from using state resources for campaigning.

  • ECI sets phase-wise voting dates
  • MCC prohibits new policy announcements
  • Notification published in Official Gazette
how_to_reg

Step 2 · Voter rolls updated

Voter Registration & Electoral Roll

Citizens register on voter.eci.gov.in using Form 6. Electoral Rolls are maintained by BLOs (Booth Level Officers). The EPIC card is the primary voter identity document.

  • Age 18+ Indian citizens eligible
  • EPIC (Voter ID) issued by ECI
  • 12 alternative documents accepted at booths
assignment_ind

Step 3 · Candidate filing

Nomination, Scrutiny & Withdrawal

Candidates file nomination papers with the Returning Officer. A ₹25,000 security deposit is required (₹12,500 for SC/ST).

  • Lok Sabha: Indian citizen, age 25+
  • Criminal & financial affidavit mandatory
  • Party symbols allotted by ECI
how_to_vote

Step 4 · Election Day

Voting via EVM & VVPAT

Voters cast ballots using Electronic Voting Machines. Each EVM has a VVPAT showing the chosen candidate on a paper slip for 7 seconds. NOTA option available for all voters.

  • Polling hours: 7AM to 6PM
  • NOTA: None of the Above — available since 2013
  • Central forces deployed for security
verified

Step 5 · Counting Day

Vote Counting & Result Certification

Counting begins at 8AM at counting centers. Postal ballots counted first, then EVM votes. India uses First Past The Post (FPTP) — highest votes wins.

  • Postal ballots counted before EVM results
  • Deposit forfeited below 1/6th of valid votes
  • ECI issues election certificate to winner
