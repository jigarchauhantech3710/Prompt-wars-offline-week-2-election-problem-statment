The Election Timeline

From initial planning to certified results — every phase of a democratic election explained clearly.

app_registration

Phase 1 · Months Before Election

Voter Registration

Citizens register to vote, confirming their eligibility and address. Deadlines vary by location — typically 15–30 days before election day.

  • Verify citizenship and age requirements (usually 18+)
  • Register online, by mail, or in person at a government office
  • Confirm registration status before the deadline
  • Update registration if you've moved or changed name
assignment_ind

Phase 2 · Months Before Election

Candidate Filing & Primaries

Candidates officially declare their candidacy and file with election authorities. Primary elections narrow down candidates from each party.

  • Candidates submit nomination papers and filing fees
  • Eligibility is verified (age, citizenship, residency)
  • Primary elections held for party selection
  • Ballots are finalized with confirmed candidates
campaign

Phase 3 · Weeks–Months Before Election

Campaigning Period

Candidates and parties campaign to win voter support through debates, rallies, advertising, and community outreach.

  • Public debates and town halls allow voter Q&A
  • Campaign finance rules regulate spending and donations
  • Media advertising across TV, digital, and print
  • Canvassing and get-out-the-vote drives
edit_calendar

Phase 4 · Days–Weeks Before Election Day

Early Voting & Absentee Ballots

Many jurisdictions allow voting before Election Day to improve accessibility and reduce polling place congestion.

  • Request absentee/mail-in ballot (if applicable)
  • Early in-person voting opens at designated locations
  • Mail ballots must be returned by the stated deadline
  • Track your ballot submission status online
how_to_vote

Phase 5 · Election Day

Election Day Voting

Registered voters cast their ballots at designated polling places. Poll workers ensure the process is fair, secure, and accessible.

  • Find your polling place using your registration address
  • Bring required ID (requirements vary by jurisdiction)
  • Cast your ballot privately in a voting booth
  • Poll watchers from parties observe the process
  • Polls close at a fixed time — be in line before closing
calculate

Phase 6 · After Polls Close

Vote Counting & Canvassing

Ballots are counted by election officials, results are verified, and the official canvass confirms the totals before certification.

  • Machines and/or hand count ballots under observer oversight
  • Provisional ballots reviewed for eligibility
  • Unofficial results released on election night
  • Official canvass verifies all counts over days/weeks
  • Recounts triggered if margin falls within legal threshold
verified

Phase 7 · Weeks After Election

Certification & Transition

The canvassed results are certified by election authorities, winners are declared, and the transition of power begins.

  • State/local authorities certify the official results
  • Any legal challenges heard in court
  • Winners notified and transition teams activated
  • Inauguration or swearing-in ceremony takes place

Key Terms

Election Glossary

Essential vocabulary every informed voter should know.

Ballot
The official document (paper or electronic) on which voters record their choices for candidates or issues.
Canvass
The official process of reviewing and totaling all votes cast in an election before certifying results.
Electoral College
A body of representatives (electors) who formally cast votes to elect the U.S. President and Vice President.
Gerrymandering
Manipulating district boundaries to give one political party an advantage in elections.
Incumbent
The current holder of a political office who is running for re-election.
Precinct
A defined geographic subdivision used for election administration and assigning voters to polling places.
Primary Election
A preliminary election in which voters choose a party's candidate to advance to the general election.
Provisional Ballot
A safeguard ballot used when a voter's eligibility cannot be confirmed on Election Day; reviewed after polls close.
Recount
A repeat tabulation of votes in an election, triggered when results are extremely close or contested.
Runoff Election
A second election held between the top candidates when no candidate wins a required majority in the first election.
Suffrage
The right to vote in political elections. Universal suffrage means all adult citizens may vote regardless of characteristics.
Swing State
A state or constituency where no single candidate or party has overwhelming support, making it a key battleground.
